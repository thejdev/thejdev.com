Expanding Water and Sanitation

in the Solomon Islands

By Jaidev Vasudevan

Adapted from an article published in Water Engineers Australia, July 2012

The Solomon Islands has experienced a tumultuous decade, with ethnic tensions escalating over a five-year period from 1998. With the support of the Regional Assistance Mission to Solomon Islands (RAMSI), hostilities ceased in 2003 and the country began the rebuilding process. Today, essential services such as education, health and infrastructure, which were disrupted by the tensions, are slowly regaining momentum. Numerous challenges lay ahead, particularly for infrastructure projects such as water and sanitation. However the outlook is optimistic.

The majority of the population engage in subsistence farming and fishing, and have little disposable income. Although many of these communities were geographically removed from the tensions, the effect was widespread disruption of government services.
